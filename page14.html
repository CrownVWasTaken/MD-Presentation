Surprising or interesting things about Muscular Dystrophy

The group is working on a novel technology known as "Cure DMD (Cure Duchenne MD)." By modifying the genes that encode or trigger the formation of dystrophin, it is hoped to treat DMD before birth. Because it is X-linked, MD is more common in boys. This indicates that a boy child's mother contracts the sickness. MD can be inherited from any one of a woman's two X chromosomes. However, a carrier with a single X chromosomal abnormality might not exhibit any MD signs at all.
